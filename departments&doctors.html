Departments

Departments & Services Offered




The General Practitioner is your first point of contact for medical care and offers a full range of healthcare services, from regular checkups to the identification and treatment of common illnesses.

* Physical examinations and regular checkups.
* Identifying and treating frequent injuries and illnesses.
* Prescribing medicines and treatments.
* Offering immunizations and vaccinations.
* Directing specialized care and managing persistent conditions.
* Providing advice on healthy living and health education.
* Sending patients to specialists when appropriate.
* Screenings for various medical conditions.
* Discussing minor surgical procedures and wound care.


The design and fitting of orthopedic devices to support and improve the body's mobility is the specialty of the orthotist, who also ensures comfortable movement and an improved quality of life.

* Constructing and designing orthopedic supports and braces.
* Orthotic devices can be made to order to enhance comfort and mobility.
* Assessing the need for orthotic intervention in patients.
* Ensuring that orthoses are properly fitted and adjusted for maximum effectiveness.
* Working together to provide comprehensive care with other medical professionals.
* Addressing musculoskeletal disorders and conditions.
* Monitoring and keeping track of patients' progress while receiving orthotic treatment.


Through specialized exercises and therapies, the physiotherapist focuses on restoring physical function and mobility, assisting in injury recovery and enhancing general wellbeing.

* Creating individualized exercise and recovery programs.
* By using manual therapy techniques, we can reduce pain and increase mobility. recovering from operations, accidents, or injuries.
* Offering techniques for pain management.
* Taking care of neuromuscular and musculoskeletal conditions.
* Enhancing flexibility, range of motion, and posture.
* Offering ergonomic guidance for avoiding injuries.
* Identifying and treating movement dysfunctions.
* Teaching patients how to take care of themselves.
* Improving conditioning and performance in sports.


The audiologist is an expert in identifying and treating hearing and balance issues and offers tailored solutions to enhance communication and auditory health. .


* Conducting thorough hearing assessments and evaluations.
* Adjusting and setting up hearing aids for optimum performance.
* Providing information and prevention methods for hearing loss.
* Supplying individualized tinnitus management solutions.
* Performing balance evaluations and vestibular rehabilitation.
* Collaborating with other experts in the field to provide complete care.
* Supporting and assisting with cochlear implant evaluations.
* Conducting newborn hearing tests.
* Providing communication training and auditory rehabilitation.
* Supplying technologies and aids for people with hearing impairments.


The chiropractor's primary goal is to realign the musculoskeletal system of the body to encourage natural healing and relieve pain. This is done through manual adjustments and other therapies.


* Performing manual manipulations and spinal adjustments.
* Tackling musculoskeletal discomfort and pain.
* Treating joint, back, and neck discomfort.
* Strengthening the posture and spinal alignment.
* Providing methods for rehabilitation and pain management.
* Giving dietary and lifestyle advice.
* Helping with sports injuries and improving performance.
* Educating patients about spinal health and injury avoidance.
* Collaborating with additional medical specialists when necessary.
* Using chiropractic treatment to advance general health.


The Psychologist provides expert counseling and therapy to support mental and emotional health, assisting people in overcoming obstacles and leading satisfying lives.


* Counseling and therapy sessions for individuals.
* Identifying and treating different mental health issues.
* Providing other forms of therapy, such as cognitive behavioral therapy.
* Addressing trauma, stress, anxiety, and depression.
* Helping people develop coping mechanisms and emotional control.
* Evaluating and conducting psychological tests.
* Providing family and couple's therapy.
* Giving psychoeducational information on mental health issues.
* Collaborating with other medical professionals to provide integrated care.
* Fostering resilience and mental health.


A healthy set of teeth and gums are essential for good oral hygiene, which is why the dentist offers treatments in addition to regular checkups.


* Performing regular dental examinations and cleanings.
* Identifying and treating dental conditions like cavities and gum disease.
* Offering services in cosmetic dentistry, such as veneers and teeth whitening.
* Providing restorative procedures like fillings, crowns, and bridges.
* Administering root canal treatment and tooth extractions.
* Putting in and taking care of dental appliances like braces and dentures.
* Teaching patients proper oral hygiene techniques.
* Offering dental sealants and preventive care.
* Conducting oral cancer examinations.
* Working in partnership with experts on challenging dental cases.


The dietitian provides individualized nutritional counseling and plans to advance healthy eating practices and effectively manage a variety of medical conditions.


* Dietary plans and individually tailored nutritional evaluations.
* Advice on how to manage your weight and eat healthfully.
* Diets tailored for conditions like diabetes and heart disease.
* Nutritional tips for optimum athletic performance.
* Eating disorders and disordered eating counseling.
* Educating people about nutrition to promote wellness as a whole.
* Assistance with food intolerances and allergies.
* Controlling portions and preparing a balanced diet.
* Collaborating with medical teams to provide complete care.
* Encouraging a sustainable and healthy diet.
